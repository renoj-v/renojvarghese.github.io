Subconscious: Mental Health Storytelling Platform Redesign

introduction

Subsconsious is a non-profit dedicated to telling real people's mental health stories. My mission was redesigning the initial experience readers have with Subconscious's web platform. The first touch is important to convince the reader the value of mental health storytelling.

Goal 1

Simplify finding mental health stories and resources.

Goal 2

Introduce the Subconscious organization, its mission, and history.

Goal 3

Showcase how donations support Subconcious's mission.

Overview

Role: UX Researcher, UX Designer

Team: The Subconscious Team: Cathy Liu (Operations), Allison Chang (Operations), Robin Kuang (Design Mentor), and Jennifer Chiang (Content)

Contributions: User Testing, Personas, Journey Maps, Final Redesigns

Duration: 10 Weeks

Stories are the stars of the website

Make it easier to search and filter articles based on terms people would search for.

Talk about how and why Subconscious started

Let's increase transparency and introduce the organization by telling where it started and why its founders pursued this mission of storytelling.

Be clear how donations support the mission

Show where donation funds go so that readers can understand why they should support Subconscious.

research

Research on UserTesting.com

The first step in the process was conducting user tests with real people. My main tool was UserTesting.com that provided video and audio recording of people using our site

Key Insights and Opportunities

Finding a story is challenging without navigation or search tools

Maybe we could make mental health stories easier to find

People want to learn more about the Subconscious organization

Maybe we could build trust by sharing more information about Subconscious

Readers are hesistant to donate because they are unsure where Donations go

Maybe we could redesign how donations are presented to be more transparent

Personas and Journey Maps

Personas and Journey Maps helped summarize my research results to the Subconscious organization and guide my design work later. With these I was able to have a conversation with organization leaders on how we could better introduce Subconscious to new readers

Frequent Stakeholder Checkins

A key element in the design and research process was frequently meeting with members of the operations, design, and content. Their feedback helped me understand the aspirations for Subconscious and incorporate the team's ideas in the final redesign

design

Sketches and Concepts

The next step was to hit the ground running and think of solutions. The best tool for this was paper and pen

High Fidelity Prototype

Since there was an already established design system, I could skip the wireframes and move onto creating a high fidelity prototype. With 2-3 iterations and checking with stakeholders, I was ready to test our new experience.

Goal 1

Simplify finding mental health stories and resources.

Proposed Solution
  • Include a search feature that could suggest topics to read about.
Rationale
  • 4/7 participants failed to find the story they were looking for.
  • Provide a search tool that gives readers an idea of what they can find.
Goal 2

Introduce the Subconscious organization, its mission, and history.

Proposed Solution
  • Redesign the About Us page to include how Subconsious started and its storytelling mission.
  • Update Get Help page to provide a reliable source of mental health resources.
Rationale
  • 5/7 Participants had repeated questions about why Subconscious started.
Goal 3

Showcase how donations support Subconcious's mission.

Proposed
  • In-depth Look at how donations are spent.
Rationale
  • 5/7 participants discussed questions about where donation money goes
  • This uncertainty made them less likely to donate.

A/B Testing

With the redesign ready, it was time to test. Using UserTesting.com again, I conducted two sets of user test focusing on how we could present the Donations page. One prototype contained a Donations page with explicit percentages and another with imagery.

Testing Results

Readers were as likely to donate with version A as with version B

There were no significant differences between how readers responded to both versions of the donation page

Participants reported they were 40% more likely to donate compared to the original site

For both the original site and the redesign participants were asked to rate how likely they would be to donate to Subconscious. The average ratings for the redesign was 40% higher than for the original site.

No issues found finding information about donations or about the Subconscious organization

The participants in the redesign reported no problems finding information throughout the site.

conclusion

Summary

  • Told the story of Subconscious, a mission driven non-profit dedicated to people's mental health stories.
  • Solved navigation issues to find mental health stories through search tools
  • Updated Get Help page to help readers find trusted, reliable mental health information from experts
  • Developed the About Us page to show readers how the organization started
  • Increased clarity and transparency about donations and Subconscious.

Challenges and Lessons

CopyWriting

When redesigning the site, I focused more on layout and hierarchy, using dummy text for copy. But to test with users, I needed more realistic copy for readers to get an understanding of who Subconscious is The Operations and Content teams volunteered to do copywriting, while I could focus on the design.
