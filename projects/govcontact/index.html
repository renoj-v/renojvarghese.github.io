GovContact

introduction

Contacting your legislator is one of the simplest and effective ways to use your political voice. GovContact puts that power at a citizen's fingertips.

Goal 1

Inform citizens about representatives' contact info and politcal stance

Goal 2

Keep citizens up to date with representatives’ actions and policies

Goal 3

Encourage citizens to send frequent, clear, and concise messages

research

Focus Group

To hit the ground running, I arranged a focus group to receive a large amount of qualitative insights in a short amount of time. Participants were asked to imagine a scenario in which they were looking to contact a representative about education reform. Then participants indivdually went to go find that information. Afterwards, there was a group discussion about their experiences.

It would be nice to have all my representatives' information in one place on my phone, kind of like having my Senator's phone number in my Contacts

– Emily

Interviews with Government Offices

To tackle the problem from another angle, I contacted my own reps. I called their offices and conducted surveys on how a citizen could best get their voice heard. It was helpful to understand what happens to a citizen's message.

Takeaways

  • Employees in representatives' offices respond to citizens' messages rather than representatives themselves

  • A citizen's message would be passed on to a representative or included as part of a summary report

  • The best way for a citizen's voice to be heard is frequent, clear, and concise messages

Online Research

To understand civic engagement on a broader scale, I looked online for previous research. A few studies, surveys, and articles revealed some new insights as well as why this problem was worth solving. Sources included The Annenberg Public Policy Center, Pew Research Center, Congress Management Foundation, The Boston Globe, and GlobalCitizen.org.

The Benenson Strategy Group in Washington, D.C., surveyed 1,000 people and found 77% could not name one of their state senators.

– Emeralde Jensen Roberts, Boston Globe

70% of communication staffers ranked personalized messages (emails, phone calls, letters) as "Very Important" towards understanding constituents opinions.

– Congress Management Foundation, "Communicating with Congress"

Competitive Analysis

There are other organizations trying to accomplish the same goal. Some analysis showed what could be improved in civic engagement apps. A few factors include the number of interactions (clicks, scroll) to get to contact info, the levels of representatives (federal, state, and local) shown, and evaluation of features.

Takeaways

  • One useful feature in the Open States and Capitol Call app was to be able to see current bills on the docket. Knowing the bills a representative has written says a lot about what the representative supports.

  • The apps that required the greatest number of intermediate interactions had a long sign-up process, surveys, or account verifications.

Design Evolution

There were 3 iterations of design and testing to get towards the final solution. At each stage, I incorporated feedback from users and mentors. Here's a brief overview of some the stages. Testing consisted of citizens using a prototype in an interview format and answering followup questions to understand their experience. Mentors provided more formal critique.

Representative Cards

Cycle through individual cards to meet reps and learn how to contact them.

Rationale

Based on the research, the main problem to solve was consoldiating representatives’ contact info in one place. Citizens could learn about reps through social media links

Feedback

If only contact info is present, it’s nicer to have them laid out one screen rather than cycling through one by one. No participants thought they would need other forms of contact information (office address, rss feed)

Single Page Layout

See representative information on a single screen. Easy to scroll through and meet all representatives. Also explored visual design

Rationale

Feedback from previous user test showed that citizens wanted to be able to meet their representatives faster. Explored visual design based on a “patriotic” theme.

Feedback

Citizens needed more information on representatives’ political stance. Party affiliation is not enough and may send a divisive message.

Representative Profiles

Profiles contain both contact info and individualized information about political stance.

Rationale

Profiles allow citizens to understand how a representative might vote. Pulled away from a single page layout to chunk more appropriate information. Began to use purple as a primary color to symbolize unity (mix of red and blue)

Feedback

Citizens would want to stay updated with what representatives are up to on a daily basis. Based on the online research and competitive analysis, it would be informative to know what legislation representatives are voting on. Another factor to consider was being able to message representatives through GovContact

Adding Political Feed and Messaging

Citizens can stay updated with representatives through a political feed, that finds representatives’ social media, petitions, and recent legislation. To avoid bias, information does not come from a formal news outlet. GovContact also has a messaging feature, which citizens can send text, voice, and video messages to representatives.

Rationale

Incorporate a more informative role for GovContact. The goal was to help citizens know what they’re represenatives are up to as well encourage them to engage with petitions and bills. The messaging system can be acceessed from representative profiles, the political feed, and the main navigation to encourage citizens to contact their representatives through GovConctact

Feedback

Engagements from social media, petitions, and bills could be sent to representatives as a summary report to be used to understand citizens’ opinions.

User Flows from Personas

To consider how well my solution would work, I considered the paths that Terry and Briana would take to accomplish their goals. Both need to learn where their representatives stand on the issues. Terry could directly message his state representatives voting on the free tuition bill. Briana could stay updated with her representatives, message representatives immediately, and share GovContact with her advocacy group.
